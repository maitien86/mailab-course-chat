## 1. Course Information
**Course Code:** IS115  
**Course Title:** Algorithms & Programming  
**Academic Term:** AY2025/26, Term 2  
** Starting date:** 12 Jan 2026 (Week 1)

**Prerequisites:**  
- High-school mathematics (algebra, logarithms, AP/GP)  
- Proficiency in non-object-oriented Python programming  
- Completion of COR-IS1704 Computational Thinking & Programming  

**Course Description:**  
This course introduces the foundations of algorithmic problem solving, data structures, and computational reasoning. Students learn to formulate problems, design and analyze algorithms, compare complexity, and implement solutions in Python. Emphasis is placed on conceptual reasoning, efficiency, and scalability.

## 3. Course Learning Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
- Design algorithms to solve computational problems  
- Analyze and compare time complexities  
- Implement algorithmic solutions in Python  
- Use fundamental data structures (stacks, queues, trees, heaps, graphs)  
- Understand computational intractability and heuristic reasoning  

---

## 4. Course Pedagogy
This course adopts a **flipped classroom model** from Week 2 onward. Students are expected to:
- Watch assigned video lectures before class  
- Complete self-check quizzes before each lesson  

Typical preparation time: **3–4 hours per week**.

---

## 5. Weekly Schedule / Course Outline

### Weeks 1–4: Algorithm Design & Analysis
1. **Week 1** – Introduction, Counting  
2. **Week 2** – Complexity  
3. **Week 3** – Iteration & Decomposition  
4. **Week 4** – Recursion  

### Weeks 5–9: Fundamental Data Structures
5. **Week 5** – Stacks, Queues  
6. **Week 6** – Binary Trees  
7. **Week 7** – Heaps & Hash Maps  
8. **Week 8** – Mid-term Break  
9. **Week 9** – Graphs  

### Weeks 10–13: Computational Intractability & Heuristic Reasoning
10. **Week 10** – Graph Algorithms  
11. **Week 11** – Heuristic Reasoning  
12. **Week 12** – No Class (Project Week)  
13. **Week 13** – Limits of Computation + Review

### Final Exam (40%)
- Covers all course topics  
- There might be questions covering group-project's questions
- Format follows the Midterm  
- On 21/04/2026 (TUE), from 8:30AM 1o 10:30AM
